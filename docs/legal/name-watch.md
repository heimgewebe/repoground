# RepoGround name watch

## Goal

Detect potentially conflicting uses early without paying for a commercial
watch service. A hit is not automatically a legal conflict; relevance
depends on similarity, territory, goods or services, timing, and actual
likelihood of confusion.

## Quarterly check

At least once per calendar quarter, search and record:

1. DPMAregister for `RepoGround`, `Repo Ground`, and close variants;
2. EUIPO/TMview for the same variants, especially software and technology
   services;
3. WIPO Global Brand Database for international registrations;
4. GitHub repository and organization names;
5. package registries including PyPI, npm, and crates.io;
6. major search engines and relevant domain registrations;
7. fundraising pages or social accounts presenting themselves as the
   official RepoGround project.

Automated alerts may supplement this process, but they are discovery signals,
not verified findings. The current free watch runs monthly and remains silent
when it finds no plausibly relevant new use. A relevant alert must still be
checked against the primary source and recorded below before it is treated as
project evidence.

## Record format

Add a dated entry under `docs/legal/name-watch-log/` containing:

- exact search date and database;
- query strings used;
- candidate name, owner, territory, classes or product area;
- source link or stable identifier;
- initial classification: irrelevant, monitor, or investigate;
- reason and next review date.

Do not publish unnecessary personal data. A potentially serious hit should
be verified from the primary registry before any contact or accusation.
