# RepoGround name and trademark policy

## Purpose

RepoGround is free and open-source software. The project name is kept
separately from the software license so that nobody can impersonate the
official project, redirect community donations, or distribute a changed
version while presenting it as an official RepoGround release.

This policy is intentionally permissive. It governs use of the RepoGround
name and project logos; it does not restrict any right granted by the
Apache License, Version 2.0. It does not claim that RepoGround is a
registered trademark in every or any jurisdiction.

## Current custodian

Until a neutral, non-profit community steward exists, Alexander Mohr acts
as custodian of the RepoGround project identity. The role is fiduciary in
spirit: the name is to be protected for the project community, not used to
create a paywall around the software.

## Uses allowed without permission

You may, truthfully and without implying endorsement:

- refer to RepoGround in articles, documentation, research, talks, reviews,
  bug reports, and comparisons;
- say that software uses, supports, integrates with, or is compatible with
  RepoGround;
- redistribute an unmodified official RepoGround release under its original
  name;
- use the name for non-commercial community meetings, study groups, and
  contribution events;
- create and distribute a fork, provided its primary name is distinguishable
  and it clearly states that it is modified and not an official RepoGround
  release;
- use screenshots or small excerpts of official project branding for
  truthful commentary and identification.

## Uses requiring prior permission

Permission is required before you:

- use RepoGround as the primary name of another product, service,
  organization, company, package, domain, or application;
- distribute a modified version under the sole or dominant name RepoGround;
- use project logos or presentation in a way likely to imply official
  sponsorship, certification, control, or endorsement;
- collect money, donations, sponsorships, or fees while representing the
  activity as official RepoGround fundraising;
- register RepoGround or a confusingly similar sign as a trademark,
  company name, package identity, social-media identity, or domain for an
  unrelated or competing undertaking.

## Fork naming

Forks are welcome. A fork may explain its origin using wording such as
“based on RepoGround” or “compatible with RepoGround”. Its main product name
must remain distinguishable, and prominent surfaces must disclose that the
fork is independent when it differs from the official project.

## Enforcement principles

Enforcement is limited to preventing material confusion, impersonation,
fraud, deceptive fundraising, or misattribution. The normal first step is a
clear notice and a reasonable opportunity to correct the presentation.
Good-faith community use, criticism, interoperability, and exercise of the
Apache-2.0 software freedoms are not enforcement targets.

## Community transition

When a durable non-profit community entity with transparent governance
exists, the intended path is to transfer or license stewardship of the
RepoGround identity to that entity under safeguards against sale,
enclosure, or donor control.

## Contact

Until a dedicated project contact exists, requests may be opened publicly
as a GitHub issue in `heimgewebe/repoground`. Sensitive disputes should not
include private personal information in a public issue.
